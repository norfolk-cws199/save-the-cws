Save Meadow Farm's Wildlife Meadow

Help protect this vital habitat – object by 5 June 2025

This is more than just a field

The land at the end of Meadow Farm Drive might look empty now, but it's not just any piece of grassland. It’s part of a designated County Wildlife Site (CWS 199) – a protected area that supports:

  • 🦔 Hedgehogs, bats, and otters
  • 🐦 Kingfishers, Yellowhammers, Song Thrushes & more
  • 🪻 Exceptionally rare plants like Norfolk Comfrey
  • 🦎 Amphibians and reptiles including slow worms and common toads
  • 🦇 Up to 7 species of bat feeding along the ditch and woodland edge

What’s happening?

A developer has applied to build a new house on this land (Planning ref: 2025/1376), clearing trees and hedgerows beforehand. If approved, it could set a dangerous precedent for more development within the wildlife site.

✋ Your voice matters. If you care about this landscape and wildlife, you can object.

Deadline to object: 5 June 2025

Top reasons to object:

  • ❌ The land is part of a County Wildlife Site – not suitable for housing
  • 🚧 No footpath or safe access – unsafe on a 60mph road
  • 🌧️ The land drains downhill – surface water could cause flooding
  • 🌿 Trees and hedgerows were cleared before applying – bad precedent
  • 🏡 If approved, this may open the door to more houses on protected land

How to object

Just email a short note to:

📧 planning@southnorfolkandbroadland.gov.uk

Include:

  • Your name and address
  • The planning reference: 2025/1376
  • A sentence saying you object and why (e.g. harm to wildlife or flood risk)
